Most companies treat collaboration tools like a checklist: buy the platform, roll it out, move on. The tools aren't the hard part, though. Getting people to actually change how they work day to day, when they're already buried in meetings and Slack threads and half-finished projects? That's where it falls apart.

AI-driven workflows can help with that. They take repetitive coordination off your plate so your team spends less time on process and more time on the work itself. We've seen teams cut their meeting load by a third just by automating status updates and handoffs.

That said, the tech only works if people trust it. Start small, prove it saves time, and let adoption grow from there.
