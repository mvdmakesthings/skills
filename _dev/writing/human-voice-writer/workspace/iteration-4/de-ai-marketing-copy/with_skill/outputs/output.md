Most teams already have plenty of tools. What they lack is a reason to use them together. The hard part of collaboration isn't picking software. It's getting people to change how they work.

AI-driven workflows can help with that. They take repetitive coordination off people's plates, so the team spends less time on process and more time on the actual work. We've seen teams cut their meeting load in half just by automating handoffs between stages of a project.

But none of that matters if you treat it as a tech problem. You have to build habits around the tools, not just install them.
