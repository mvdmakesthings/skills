# /writer:human — Before & After

This file demonstrates the `/writer:human` skill. The "Before" section is the original AI-generated text; the "After" section is the result of running it through the skill.

## Before

> Pizza works because it's endlessly adaptable without ever losing what makes it good in the first place. The crust gives you something to hold onto — thin and crackly, thick and chewy, stuffed, folded, whatever you're in the mood for. The sauce and cheese do the heavy lifting, but it's the toppings where people get to express themselves. You can go classic with pepperoni and mushrooms or pile on something unexpected like fig and prosciutto, and somehow the format just absorbs it all. It feeds a crowd without anyone having to agree on a single dish. It's cheap enough for a Tuesday night and good enough for a celebration. Cold leftovers at midnight hit different than almost any other food. And maybe the best part: nobody has ever walked into a room carrying pizza and been met with anything less than genuine enthusiasm.

## After

The thing about pizza is that you can do almost anything to it and it still works. Crust alone gives you a dozen directions. Thin, crackly, barely holding together. Thick and pillowy. Stuffed. Folded in half while you walk down the street. Sauce and cheese carry the whole thing, but toppings are where people start making choices. Pepperoni and mushrooms if you're playing it safe. Fig and prosciutto if you're feeling ambitious. The format doesn't care. It absorbs all of it.

And it solves the hardest part of feeding a group: nobody has to agree on one thing. Order three different pies and everyone eats what they want. It costs almost nothing on a random weeknight but still feels right at a birthday party. Cold slices out of the fridge at midnight might honestly be better than the first round.

But here's what gets me. Nobody has ever walked into a room carrying a stack of pizza boxes and been met with anything other than pure, unfiltered excitement. Every single time, people light up.

## What Changed

**Em dashes removed.** The original used an em dash ("hold onto — thin and crackly") which is one of the strongest AI writing tells. The rewrite breaks that into sentence fragments instead, which also improves rhythm.

**"Endlessly" dropped.** Hollow intensifiers like "endlessly," "truly," and "incredibly" are scattered through AI text for false emphasis. The rewrite cuts it and lets the point stand on its own.

**Sentence length variation (burstiness).** The original kept most sentences in a similar 15-25 word range. AI detection tools flag this uniformity. The rewrite mixes one-word fragments ("Stuffed.") and short punches ("The format doesn't care.") against longer compound sentences. That contrast is how people actually write.

**Broke up the single block into multiple paragraphs.** The original was one dense paragraph. The rewrite uses three paragraphs of different lengths (long, medium, short) with different openings. AI tends to produce uniform paragraph structures. Humans don't.

**Varied paragraph openings.** Each paragraph starts differently: a declarative statement, an "And" connector, a "But" pivot. The original read more like a continuous monologue with consistent pacing.

**Swapped some phrasing for plainer language.** "A dozen directions" instead of "endlessly adaptable." "Carry the whole thing" instead of "do the heavy lifting." "Playing it safe" and "feeling ambitious" instead of "go classic" and "pile on something unexpected." These are smaller moves, but they add up to text that reads like someone talking rather than someone composing.

**Preserved all the original ideas.** The rewrite covers the same ground: crust variety, topping personalization, group-friendliness, price range, cold leftovers, and the universal excitement of pizza arriving. Nothing was added or removed in terms of content.
