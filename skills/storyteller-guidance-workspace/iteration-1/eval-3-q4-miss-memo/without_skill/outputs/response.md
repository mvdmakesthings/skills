## The Bottom Line

## What Happened

Two factors drove the gap, roughly in equal measure:

**1. The market softened in November.**
Starting in early November, we saw deal cycles extend, budget approvals stall, and a noticeable uptick in "let's revisit in Q1" responses across our pipeline. This wasn't unique to us — peers and analysts are reporting the same pattern — but it hit us at the worst possible moment in our quarter. Roughly half of the miss traces to deals that were qualified, engaged, and forecast-able that simply didn't close in the window we expected.

**2. Our pricing change underperformed our model.**
In early Q4 we rolled out the [pricing change] with the goal of [intended outcome]. The model assumed close rates would hold within a few points of historical baseline. They didn't — they dropped more than we projected, particularly in the mid-market segment where the new pricing created friction in the back half of the sales conversation. This is on the strategy, not the execution. The sales team flagged early signals, and in hindsight we should have moved faster to adjust.

I want to be clear about the second point: the team sold hard against a structure that worked against them in the final stretch. They did the job we asked them to do. The lesson is ours at the leadership level — we need tighter feedback loops between pricing decisions and frontline data.
